“Dinah’s our cat. And she’s such a capital one for catching mice you can’t think! And oh, I wish you could see her after the birds! Why, she’ll eat a little bird as soon as look at it!”
This speech caused a remarkable sensation among the party.
 Some of the birds hurried off at once.
 One old Magpie began wrapping itself up very carefully, remarking, “I really must be getting home; the night-air doesn’t suit my throat!”
 A Canary called out in a trembling voice to its children, “Come away, my dears! It’s high time you were all in bed!”
 On various pretexts, they all moved off, and I was soon left alone.
“I wish I hadn’t mentioned Dinah!”
 I said to myself in a melancholy tone.
