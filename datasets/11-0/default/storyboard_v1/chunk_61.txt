and while I was peering about anxiously among the trees, a little sharp bark just over my head made me look up in a great hurry.
An enormous puppy was looking down at me with large round eyes, and feebly stretching out one paw, trying to touch me.
 “Poor little thing!”
 I said, in a coaxing tone, and I tried hard to whistle to it;
 but I was terribly frightened all the time at the thought that it might be hungry, in which case it would be very likely to eat me up in spite of all my coaxing.
Hardly knowing what I did, I picked up a little bit of stick, and held it out to the puppy;
 whereupon the puppy jumped into the air off all its feet at once, with a yelp of delight, and rushed at the stick, and made believe to worry it;
 then I dodged behind a great thistle, to keep myself from being run over;
