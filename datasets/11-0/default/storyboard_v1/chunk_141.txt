“And so these three little sisters—they were learning to draw, you know—”
“What did they draw?”
 I asked, quite forgetting my promise.
“Treacle,”
 said the Dormouse, without considering at all this time.
“I want a clean cup,”
 interrupted the Hatter: “let’s all move one place on.”
He moved on as he spoke, and the Dormouse followed him: the March Hare moved into the Dormouse’s place, and I rather unwillingly took the place of the March Hare.
