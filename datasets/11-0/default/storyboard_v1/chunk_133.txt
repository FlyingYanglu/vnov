The Hatter sighed, “Yes, that’s it. It’s always tea-time, and we’ve no time to wash the things between whiles.”
“Then you keep moving round, I suppose?”
 I said.
 “Exactly so,”
 the Hatter replied, “as the things get used up.”
“But what happens when you come to the beginning again?”
 I ventured to ask.
 The March Hare interrupted, yawning, “Suppose we change the subject. I’m getting tired of this. I vote the young lady tells us a story.”
