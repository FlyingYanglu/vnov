 either the locks were too large, or the key was too small, but at any rate it would not open any of them.
 However, on the second time round, I came upon a low curtain I had not noticed before, and behind it was a little door about fifteen inches high: I tried the little golden key in the lock, and to my great delight it fitted!
I opened the door and found that it led into a small passage, not much larger than a rat-hole.
 I knelt down and looked along the passage into the loveliest garden I ever saw.
 How I longed to get out of that dark hall and wander about among those beds of bright flowers and those cool fountains.
 But I could not even get my head through the doorway.
