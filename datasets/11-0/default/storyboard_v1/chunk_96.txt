I said politely, feeling pleased to have started a conversation.
“You don’t know much,”
 the Duchess retorted, “and that’s a fact.”
I didn’t like the tone of her remark, so I decided to change the subject.
 While I was trying to think of something else to say, the cook took the cauldron of soup off the fire and began throwing everything within her reach at the Duchess and the baby.
 Fire-irons, saucepans, plates, and dishes flew everywhere.
 The Duchess barely noticed, and the baby’s howling made it impossible to tell if the blows were hurting it.
“Oh, _please_ mind what you’re doing!”
