The Duchess was sitting on a three-legged stool in the middle, nursing a baby.
 The cook was leaning over the fire, stirring a large cauldron that seemed to be full of soup.
“There’s certainly too much pepper in that soup!”
 I thought to myself, trying to suppress a sneeze.
The air was thick with pepper, making it hard to breathe.
 Even the Duchess sneezed occasionally, and the baby alternated between sneezing and howling without a moment’s pause.
 The only things in the kitchen that did not sneeze were the cook and a large cat sitting on the hearth, grinning from ear to ear.
