The poor thing was waving its tail about in a melancholy way, unable to move.
 I soon got it out again and put it right, though I thought to myself, "Not that it signifies much; I should think it would be _quite_ as much use in the trial one way up as the other."
Once the jurymen had recovered from the shock and their slates and pencils were found and handed back to them, they set to work very diligently to write out a history of the accident.
 All except the Lizard, who seemed too much overcome to do anything but sit with its mouth open, gazing up into the roof of the court.
"What do you know about this business?"
 the King asked me.
"Nothing,"
 I replied.
