“If any one of them can explain it,”
 I said, (I had grown so large in the last few minutes that I wasn’t a bit afraid of interrupting him,) “I’ll give him sixpence. _I_ don’t believe there’s an atom of meaning in it.”
The jury all wrote down on their slates, “_She_ doesn’t believe there’s an atom of meaning in it,”
 but none of them attempted to explain the paper.
“If there’s no meaning in it,”
 said the King, “that saves a world of trouble, you know, as we needn’t try to find any. And yet I don’t know,”
 he went on, spreading out the verses on his knee, and looking at them with one eye;
 “I seem to see some meaning in them, after all. “
