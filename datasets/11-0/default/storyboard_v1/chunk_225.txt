 I said this last word two or three times over to myself, being rather proud of it: for I thought, and rightly too, that very few little girls of my age knew the meaning of it at all.
 However, “jury-men”
 would have done just as well.
The twelve jurors were all writing very busily on slates.
 “What are they doing?”
 I whispered to the Gryphon.
 “They can’t have anything to put down yet, before the trial’s begun.”
