A mouse—of a mouse—to a mouse—a mouse—O mouse!”) The Mouse looked at me rather inquisitively, and seemed to me to wink with one of its little eyes, but it said nothing.“
Perhaps it doesn’t understand English,” I thought; “
I daresay it’s a French mouse, come over with William the Conqueror.
” (For, with all my knowledge of history, I had no very clear notion how long ago anything had happened.) So I began again: “
Où est ma chatte?
” which was the first sentence in my French lesson-book. The Mouse gave a sudden leap out of the water, and seemed to quiver all over with fright. “
Oh, I beg your pardon!
” I cried hastily, afraid that I had hurt the poor animal’s feelings. “
