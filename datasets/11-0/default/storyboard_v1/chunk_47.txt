I was so much frightened that I ran off at once in the direction it pointed to, without trying to explain the mistake it had made.
“He took me for his housemaid,”
 I said to myself as I ran.
 “How surprised he’ll be when he finds out who I am! But I’d better take him his fan and gloves—that is, if I can find them.”
 As I said this, I came upon a neat little house, on the door of which was a bright brass plate with the name “W. RABBIT,”
 engraved upon it.
 I went in without knocking, and hurried upstairs, in great fear lest I should meet the real Mary Ann, and be turned out of the house before I had found the fan and gloves.
