As soon as I was small enough to get through the door, I ran out of the house, and found quite a crowd of little animals and birds waiting outside.
 The poor little Lizard, Bill, was in the middle, being held up by two guinea-pigs, who were giving it something out of a bottle.
 They all made a rush at me the moment I appeared;
 but I ran off as hard as I could, and soon found myself safe in a thick wood.
“The first thing I’ve got to do,”
 I said to myself, as I wandered about in the wood, “is to grow to my right size again; and the second thing is to find my way into that lovely garden. I think that will be the best plan.”
 It sounded an excellent plan, no doubt, and very neatly and simply arranged;
 the only difficulty was, that I had not the smallest idea how to set about it;
