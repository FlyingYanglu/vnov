I watched as they slipped the guinea-pig head first into a large canvas bag, tying it up at the mouth with strings.
 “I’m glad I’ve seen that done,”
 I thought.
 “I’ve so often read in the newspapers, at the end of trials, ‘There was some attempts at applause, which was immediately suppressed by the officers of the court,’ and I never understood what it meant till now.”
“If that’s all you know about it, you may stand down,”
 the King continued.
“I can’t go no lower,”
 the Hatter said, “I’m on the floor, as it is.”
