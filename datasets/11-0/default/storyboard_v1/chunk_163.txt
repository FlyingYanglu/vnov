I began to feel very uneasy: to be sure, I had not as yet had any dispute with the Queen, but I knew that it might happen any minute, “and then,”
 I thought, “what would become of me? They’re dreadfully fond of beheading people here; the great wonder is, that there’s any one left alive!”
I was looking about for some way of escape, and wondering whether I could get away without being seen, when I noticed a curious appearance in the air: it puzzled me very much at first, but, after watching it a minute or two, I made it out to be a grin, and I said to myself “It’s the Cheshire Cat: now I shall have somebody to talk to.”
“How are you getting on?”
 said the Cat, as soon as there was mouth enough for it to speak with.
