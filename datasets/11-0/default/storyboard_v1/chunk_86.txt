“It matters a good deal to _me_,”
 I said hastily;
 “but I’m not looking for eggs, as it happens; and if I was, I shouldn’t want _yours_: I don’t like them raw.”
“Well, be off, then!”
 the Pigeon said in a sulky tone, as it settled down again into its nest.
 I crouched down among the trees as well as I could, for my neck kept getting entangled among the branches, and every now and then I had to stop and untwist it.
 After a while, I remembered that I still held the pieces of mushroom in my hands, and I set to work very carefully, nibbling first at one and then at the other, and growing sometimes taller and sometimes shorter, until I had succeeded in bringing myself down to my usual height.
