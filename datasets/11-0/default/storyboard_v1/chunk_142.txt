The Hatter was the only one who got any advantage from the change: and I was a good deal worse off than before, as the March Hare had just upset the milk-jug into his plate.
I did not wish to offend the Dormouse again, so I began very cautiously: “But I don’t understand. Where did they draw the treacle from?”
“You can draw water out of a water-well,”
 said the Hatter;
 “so I should think you could draw treacle out of a treacle-well—eh, stupid?”
“But they were _in_ the well,”
 I said to the Dormouse, not choosing to notice this last remark.
