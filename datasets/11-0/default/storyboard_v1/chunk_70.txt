I decided to wait, as I had nothing else to do, and perhaps it might tell me something worth hearing.
 For some minutes, the Caterpillar puffed away without speaking.
 Finally, it unfolded its arms, took the hookah out of its mouth again, and said, “So you think you’re changed, do you?”
“I’m afraid I am, sir,”
 I said;
 “I can’t remember things as I used—and I don’t keep the same size for ten minutes together!”
“Can’t remember _what_ things?”
 the Caterpillar asked.
