Hardly knowing what I did, I picked up a little bit of stick, and held it out to the puppy; whereupon the puppy jumped into the air off all its feet a
t once, with a yelp of delight, and rushed at the stick, and made believe to worry it; then I dodged behind a great thistle, to keep myself from being
run over; and the moment I appeared on the other side, the puppy made another rush at the stick, and tumbled head over heels in its hurry to get hold
of it; then I, thinking it was very like having a game of play with a cart-horse, and expecting every moment to be trampled under its feet, ran round
