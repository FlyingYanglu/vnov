He looked at me, Alice, and tried to speak, but for a minute or two sobs choked his voice.
The Gryphon, ever the impatient one, set to work shaking him and punching him in the back.
At last, the Mock Turtle recovered his voice, and with tears running down his cheeks, he began to speak again.
“You may not have lived much under the sea—” he started, and I quickly interjected, “I haven’t.”
