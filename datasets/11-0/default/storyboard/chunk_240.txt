“They couldn’t have done that, you know,” I gently remarked, “they’d have been ill.”
“So they were,” the Dormouse said, “_very_ ill.”

I tried to fancy to myself what such an extraordinary way of living would be like, but it puzzled me
too much, so I went on, “But why did they live at the bottom of a well?”

“Take some more tea,” the March Hare said to me, very earnestly.
“I’ve had nothing yet,” I replied in an offended tone, “so I can’t take more.”
