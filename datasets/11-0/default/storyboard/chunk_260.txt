The other gardeners looked around, and all of them bowed low.
“Would you tell me,” I said, a little timidly, “why you are painting those roses?”

Five and Seven said nothing, but looked at Two.
Two began in a low voice, “Why the fact is, you see, Miss, this here ought to have been a red rose-tree, and we put a white one in by mistake; and if
the Queen was to find it out, we should all have our heads cut off, you know.
